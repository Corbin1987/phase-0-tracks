Let's review some favorite elements!

Carbon: This element is essential to everyday life. There is an entire field of study devoted to how carbon works with other elements! Unfortunately, this field of study is often the bane of existence for many aspiring chemistry majors. The versatility of carbon, with its ability to create chains of itself into a near infinite variety of molecules, makes this element fascinating.
  • Atomic number: 6
  • Atomic weight: 12.0107
  • Electron shell structure: 1s2 2s2 2p2
Some pictures of carbon
Gold: The symbol for gold, Au, comes from the Latin word "Aurum." Gold has a variety of uses in electronics, medicine, art, and currency, to name a few areas. Gold, being a heavy element, can be formed as the result of supernova nucleosynthesis.
  • Atomic number: 79
  • Atomic weight: 196.96655
  • Electron shell structure: 1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d10 5s2 5p6 4f14 5d10 6s1
Some pictures of gold
Titanium: One of the strongest metals, titanium is found in everything from dental implants to cellphones. Its resistance to corrosion also lends its usefulness in medical implants.
  • Atomic number: 22
  • Atomic weight: 47.867
  • Electron shell structure: 1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d2 4s2
Some pictures of titanium
Oxygen: Where would we be without oxygen? The essential need for this element would literally take our breath away if it didn't exist. Add two hydrogen atoms to an oxygen atom, and you get one of the most important molecules on Earth.
  • Atomic number: 8
  • Atomic weight: 15.9994
  • Electron shell structure: 1s2 2s2 2p4
Some pictures of oxygen
